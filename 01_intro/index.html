Many times, our circles turn out to be ovals or have disconnected starting and ending points, or vary in pressure halfway through, resulting in an opaque semi-circle. Maybe after 10 tries we'll get the perfect one. Getting an accurate and proportionate circle is quite important when it comes to drawing anatomy and facial features, so having an artist glove that will do it for you is innovative and a time saver. How I envision it'll work is that the cloth glove (or perhaps a mold cast created exclusively for the wearer) will extend from mid-thumb, -index, and -middle finger, all the way down to about 2 inches below the wrist. There will be a microcontroller programmer attached to the top of the wrist, coded with different circle sizes. The wearer can choose whichever option they want, and then the program will send a signal to a sort of motor or rotating mini device that will follow along the circumference of the circle. Before it draws the circle, once the option is clicked and ready to go, the wearer must pick up their drawing utensil, fix their fingers, hand, and wrist in the desired position, and once satisifed with the position, the wearer will press a button or controller that will lock the glove in this position so the wrist does not wobble or fingers slip. How to do that, I was thinking on each joint there will be little snaps in place that will be snapped shut through wires upon the button being pressed. On the finger parts will be placed rubber soles for extra adhesiveness the drawing utensil. </div>
